Chick-fil-A

Chick-fil-A is a popular American fast-food chain known for its chicken sandwiches, exceptional customer service, and strong corporate values. Founded in 1946 by S. Truett Cathy in Hapeville, Georgia, the company started as a small diner called the Dwarf Grill (later renamed the Dwarf House) before evolving into the Chick-fil-A brand in 1967.
